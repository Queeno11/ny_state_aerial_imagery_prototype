\section{Computer Vision and Mixed Spatial-Temporal Siamese Architecture}

To capture the full economic dynamics of the urban environment while ensuring strict out-of-sample generalizability across diverse urban typologies, our architecture is explicitly designed with a \textbf{dual objective}. First, it acts as an ordinal feature extractor to predict the relative ranking of neighborhood structural wealth at a given point in time. Second, it predicts the directional magnitude of structural change (gentrification or decay) across a multi-year panel. 

To achieve this without suffering from spatial cardinality mismatch or catastrophic forgetting, we employ a Mixed Spatial-Temporal Siamese framework. This architecture utilizes stochastic batch composition to simultaneously map the static visual hierarchy of wealth and regularize the network against temporal survey noise.

\subsection{Feature Extraction and Spatial Spillover ($\tau$)}
To isolate the temporal learning mechanism from extraneous optical noise (e.g., building shadows, changes in sun angles and lighting), the prototype model utilizes rasterized vector building footprints sourced from the official New York City Department of Information Technology and Telecommunications (DoITT). 

Because neighborhood wealth is influenced by localized externalities, the visual input for tract $i$ is not strictly bounded by its census geometry. We introduce a \textbf{spatial spillover hyperparameter, $\tau$}, defined as a buffer radius (in meters) extending beyond each building centroid. Let $X_{i,t}^\tau$ represent the multi-channel rasterized matrix of tract $i$ in year $t \in \{2008, 2010, 2012, 2014, 2016, 2018, 2020, 2022, 2024\}$ inclusive of the $\tau$-meter spillover, encoding the features latent in the aerial imagery.

We define a feature extraction sub-network, $f_\theta(\cdot)$, parameterized by weights $\theta$. This sub-network projects the physical infrastructure matrix into a lower-dimensional latent representation, $h_{i,t} \in \mathbb{R}^d$:
\begin{equation}
h_{i,t} = f_\theta(X_{i,t}^\tau)
\end{equation}

A fully connected regression head, $g_\phi(\cdot)$ with weights $\phi$, then maps this physical embedding to a continuous scalar ranking score, representing the tract's relative structural wealth position:
\begin{equation}
\hat{R}_{i,t} = g_\phi(h_{i,t})
\end{equation}

\subsection{Out-of-Sample Generalizability and Quantile Mapping}
A core methodological innovation of this architecture is the intentional discarding of spatial cardinality during cross-sectional training. Urban topologies scale differently; a one-standard-deviation increase in structural wealth in New York manifests vertically, whereas in Dallas, it manifests horizontally. By constraining the network to act strictly as a monotonic ordinal ranker within a specific city's batch, we prevent the model from overfitting to the topological density of the training data. 

To deploy this model in a purely out-of-sample city (e.g., Boston), we compute the continuous ranking scores ($\hat{R}$) for all target tracts based purely on their physical morphology. We then compute their empirical quantiles and map them directly to the corresponding quantiles of the target city's actual American Community Survey (ACS) income distribution, allowing for zero-shot econometric moment matching.

\subsection{Stochastic Batch Composition}
We utilize a stochastic weighting strategy via batch composition. Each training batch of size $B$ is explicitly stratified into three pair types to force the network to learn a universal ordinal hierarchy:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Spatial Pairs (80\%):} Pairs of distinct tracts drawn from the same city and year $(X_{i,t}^\tau, X_{j,t}^\tau)$. To prevent the network from overfitting to visual noise within dense middle-income clusters, we employ a **Curriculum Learning strategy via Threshold Annealing**. At the start of training, the Minimum Resolution Threshold ($\epsilon$) is set to a high value (e.g., $2.0\sigma$), admitting only pairs with unambiguous macro-structural differences. As training progresses, this threshold is gradually annealed following an exponential decay schedule. This allows the model to first establish a robust feature backbone on clear signals before fine-tuning on more nuanced distinctions. The threshold anneals to a final, non-zero boundary ($\epsilon_{final}$) determined by the average measurement error of the underlying ACS data, ensuring the model is never forced to learn from statistically insignificant spatial variations.
    \item \textbf{Stable Temporal Pairs (10\%):} Pairs of the \textit{same} tract across time $(X_{i,t_a}^\tau, X_{i,t_b}^\tau)$ where the econometrically cleared Macro Gate indicates no structural change ($I_i^{valid} = 0$). Crucially, for these pairs, the network ignores the natural temporal drift in their relative cross-sectional Z-scores (economic filtering) and exclusively penalizes divergence in their latent physical embeddings.
    \item \textbf{Changing Temporal Pairs (10\%):} Pairs of the \textit{same} tract across time $(X_{i,t_a}^\tau, X_{i,t_b}^\tau)$ where valid structural change occurred ($I_i^{valid} = 1$).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{The Tripartite Loss Function}
The network calculates three distinct loss penalties corresponding to the batch composition.

\vspace{0.5em}
\noindent\textbf{1. Supervised Spatial Ranking Objective:} Applied to the 80\% spatial pairs (denoted by the set $\mathbb{P}$) to evaluate cross-sectional ordinal accuracy. Let $y_{ij} = \text{sign}(L_i - L_j)$ denote the true econometric relationship between the tracts based on the year-specific ACS labels. We employ a Margin Ranking Loss with margin $m_{spatial}$:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{rank}(\theta, \phi) = \frac{1}{|\mathbb{P}|} \sum_{(i,j) \in \mathbb{P}} \max \left(0, -y_{ij} \left( \hat{R}_{i} - \hat{R}_{j} \right) + m_{spatial} \right)
\end{equation}

\vspace{0.5em}
\noindent\textbf{2. Siamese Robustness Regularization:} Applied only to the 10\% stable temporal pairs (denoted by the set $\mathbb{S}$). Because these tracts experienced no statistically significant physical change, we minimize the Euclidean distance between their latent embeddings. This forces the network to ignore spurious optical noise and macroeconomic label drift, effectively locking the structural representation:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{robust}(\theta) = \frac{1}{|\mathbb{S}|} \sum_{(i, t_a, t_b) \in \mathbb{S}} \left\| f_\theta(X_{i, t_a}^\tau) - f_\theta(X_{i, t_b}^\tau) \right\|_2^2
\end{equation}

\vspace{0.5em}
\noindent\textbf{3. Temporal Dynamic Margin Loss:} Applied only to the 10\% changing temporal pairs (denoted by the set $\mathbb{C}$). To prevent a ``lazy gradient'' where the network learns just enough to satisfy a standard ranking margin, we introduce a \textit{dynamic margin} proportional to the absolute magnitude of true econometric change: $m_{dynamic} = \alpha \left| L_{i,t_b} - L_{i,t_a} \right|$. 

Let $y_{temp} = \text{sign}(L_{i,t_b} - L_{i,t_a})$ represent the direction of gentrification or decay. The dynamic ranking loss ensures the temporal embeddings are pushed apart in proportion to the severity of the structural change:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{delta}(\theta, \phi) = \frac{1}{|\mathbb{C}|} \sum_{(i, t_a, t_b) \in \mathbb{C}} \max \left(0, -y_{temp} \left( \hat{R}_{i,t_b} - \hat{R}_{i,t_a} \right) + \alpha \left| L_{i,t_b} - L_{i,t_a} \right| \right)
\end{equation}

\subsection{Joint Loss and Hyperparameter Rescaling}
The network is trained end-to-end to minimize the total expected loss:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_{total} = \mathcal{L}_{rank}(\theta, \phi) + \lambda_1 \mathcal{L}_{robust}(\theta) + \lambda_2 \mathcal{L}_{delta}(\theta, \phi)
\end{equation}

While the 80/10/10 batch composition dictates the frequency of the gradient updates, we utilize \textbf{rescaling hyperparameters $\lambda_1$ and $\lambda_2$} to balance the magnitudes of the distinct loss spaces. These act as signal boosters, ensuring the relatively quiet temporal gradients (regularization and dynamic delta mapping) provide a sufficient push to update the network alongside the dominant spatial ranking gradients.

\subsection*{Summary of Core Architectural Hyperparameters}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{$\tau$ (Spillover Threshold):} Determines the spatial context window (in meters) beyond the tract boundary to capture localized externalities.
    \item \textbf{Batch Split $(\rho_{spatial}, \rho_{stable}, \rho_{change})$:} Set to (0.8, 0.1, 0.1) to define the stochastic probability of drawing a cross-sectional comparison versus temporal regularization pairs.
    \item \textbf{$\alpha$ (Dynamic Temporal Scale):} Maps the true magnitude of econometric change into a target mathematical margin space for the changing temporal pairs.
    \item \textbf{$\lambda_1, \lambda_2$ (Loss Weights):} Rescales the gradients of the Siamese robustness penalty and the temporal dynamic margin relative to the spatial ordinal ranker.
\end{itemize}
